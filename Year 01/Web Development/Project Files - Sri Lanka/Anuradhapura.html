Anuradhapura

This impressive ancient kingdom, where the foundation of Buddhism once thrived, still remains (even after over 23 centuries) a revered

pilgrimage centre. History reveals that the neighbouring areas of Anuradhapura were inhabited from 1 000BC and by the 5th century BC the city

developed at a rapid rate. It is said that Emperor Ashoka’s son, Mahendra, was the individual who instilled Buddhism to the island in the 3rd

century. This sacred city is acknowledged as the capital of Sri Lanka’s Buddhist way of life. Spend the day exploring myriad dagobas (mound-like

structures), remains of palaces, monasteries and temples. As Anuradhapura grew, so did the myriad shrines surrounding the ancient Bodhi tree

such as the Ruwanwelisaya Dagoba (stupa), this stark white dome was once one of the world’s tallest buildings and second only to the pyramids,

and globally Buddhists revere this hallowed site. As a significant place

of worship it has been honoured with the salutation of one of the

Solosmasthana and one of the Atamasthana, acknowledged as a place

of adoration and devotion. Explore the Jetavana Monastery, with

meticulously landscaped and preserved ruins, and learn more about

fascinating artefacts that were unearthed when the monastery was

excavated at the Jetavana museum. This Jetavana treasure includes

beads made of a variety of crystal, clay, ivory and stone, ritual objects

and paintings on slabs of stone. Delve into the monks’ living units

(Pancavasa) and the Image House where the lotus pedestal of the

Buddha image sits. A highlight of the monastery is the Jetavana stupa,

a venerable site that boasts the title of tallest brick dagoba on the

earth. This towering stupa features graceful figures of women dancing

on the eastern vahalkada (entrances to the courtyard), and the

southern side of the vahalkada presents a cobra king, a goddess and

crumbling elephant heads.

Ancient Engineering Marvels of Sri Lanka

The pride of Anuradhapura is the ancient stupas and ancient

reservoirs. Towering stupas or dagobas (pagoda type buildings) of

stupendous domes – the marvels of ancient civil engineering of Sri

Lanka were built taking into account the effects of lightening on high-

rise constructions, among numerous other engineering factors.

Other attractions at Anuradhapura are magnificent rock carvings of

monumental richness and remarkable grace, colossal stone pillars that

stand proud amidst the ruins of royal palaces etc.
