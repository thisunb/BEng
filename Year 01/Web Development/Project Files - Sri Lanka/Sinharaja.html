Sinharaja Forest

Sinharaja Forest is a tropical lowland rain forest which is located within Sabaragamuwa and Southern Provinces of the south west lowland

wetzone in Sri Lanka.It is spanning an area of total 18900 acres.Sinharaja forest contains rivers on three sides.Napola Dola and Koskulana Ganga

from the north Maha Dola and Gin Ganga from the south-southwest and on the west are the rivers Kalukanda Ela and Kudawa Ganga.

You can enter the the Sinharaja Forest Reserve from the nothern or western parts of the country via Rathnapura,Kiriella,Kalawana and

Weddala.From the south you can enter the Sinharaja Forest via Deniyaya.If you are comming from Hambanthota or Udawalawa you can enter the

forest through Rakwana. Sinharaja Forest is densly crowded with tall

trees situated very close to eachother.Winding trails make the trekking

even the trees are densly crowded in the forest. There are small

streams of crystal-clear cool water which are homes to a variety of

fish,crabs and toads. All mixed bird species are seen within the canopy

of the woods. The Annual rainfall of Sinharaja Forest is ranged between

3614mm to 5006mm and the temperature of the forest varies from

19°C to 34°C.The highest rainfall is recorded in the two monsoons

namely south-west monsoon which occurs from May-July and noth-east

monsoons which occur from November-January. The terrian of the

Sinharaja Forest consists of a series of ridges and valleys drained by a

network of streams which flow into Gin Ganga from the south and Kalu

gaga from the north via Koskulana Ganga,Kudawa Ganga and Napola

Dola.

There are two main nature trails in Sinharaja. One lead to the peak of

Moulawella and the other lead to he peak of Sinharaja. Both these

nature trails starts at Kudawa Conservation Center which is also

shortly known as KCC. Sinharaja Forest Reserve is home to over 50%

of Sri Lanka's endemic species of butteflies, mammals, insects,

repltiles as well as rare amphibians. Sinharaja rain forest is home to

numerous species of birds such as Ceylon Grey Hornbill(Ocycerous

gingalensis), Ceylon Hanging Parrot(Loriculus beryllinus), the Ashey-

headed Laughing Thrush(Garrulax cinereifrons), Jungle fowl(Gallus

lafayetii), Ceylon Wood Pigeon(Columba torringtoni), Ceylon Blue

Magpie(Urocissa ornate) etc.
